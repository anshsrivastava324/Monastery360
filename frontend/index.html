Sacred Monasteries of Sikkim

Journey through 200+ Buddhist monasteries across the Himalayan kingdom

Rumtek Monastery

📍 24 km from Gangtok | Altitude: 1,500m

The largest monastery in Sikkim, seat of the 16th Karmapa and center of the Karma Kagyu lineage. Home to golden stupas and sacred relics.

Pemayangtse Monastery

📍 Pelling, West Sikkim | Altitude: 2,085m

Founded in 1705, one of the oldest monasteries meaning "Perfect Sublime Lotus". Features a stunning 7-tiered wooden structure depicting heavenly abode.

Tashiding Monastery

📍 West Sikkim | Altitude: 1,400m

Built in 1641, "The Devoted Central Glory" sits on a hilltop between two rivers. Sacred site for the annual Bumchu festival with holy water.

Dubdi Monastery

📍 Yuksom | Altitude: 2,100m

The oldest monastery in Sikkim, built in 1701. Historical site marking the coronation of Sikkim's first Chogyal by three Lamas.

Enchey Monastery

📍 Gangtok | Altitude: 1,900m

Founded in 1840, "Sacred Stable Place" of the Nyingmapa order. Hosts spectacular masked dances during the Chaam festival.

Ralang Monastery

📍 Ravangla, South Sikkim

Sacred Kagyu monastery known for its annual Pang Lhabsol festival celebrating the guardian deity of Sikkim with warrior dances.

Phodong Monastery

📍 North Sikkim | Altitude: 1,500m

Rebuilt in the early 18th century, this Nyingmapa monastery houses 260 monks and features beautiful murals and ancient manuscripts.

Lingdum Monastery

📍 Ranka, East Sikkim

Modern monastery of the Zurmang Kagyu tradition, known for its architectural beauty and serene location amidst pine forests.
